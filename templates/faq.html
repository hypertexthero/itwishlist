Rome?

Rome is a modification (mod) of a men-with-guns simulation called Arma 2. It is arguably the game that the relatively tedious simulation was missing. Arma 2 provides large maps with forests and urban areas, accurate ballistic physics, realistic sound, day, night and weather cycles, lots of guns and vehicles. Rome provides survival gameplay (hide, scavenge, hunt), player location, inventory and condition persistence, flares, chemlights, axes, firewood, beans, multiple human-controlled players and a computer-controlled zombie horde.

To play Rome you need (currently Windows-only):

  1. A copy of the imaginatively-named Arma 2: Combined Ops.
  2. The Rome mod. There are installation instructions at Rome Wiki.

IT Wish List?

it.ippc.int is a website for people who have experienced Rome’s human-zombie apocalypse — also known as ‘survivors’ — to share their stories on the web and in-game. After signing up for an account, survivors can:

  1. Write log entries.
  2. Display profile information on the in-game Rome player roster. Survivors do this by editing their profile on it.ippc.int and pasting a generated URL to their in-game Rome settings.
  3. Follow other survivor’s logs’, comment and vote on their entries. The homepage shows the top 30.
